# LocalLeap Operations Guide

## Daily Operating System

### Morning Routine (1-2 hours)

**8:00 AM - Check & Respond**
- [ ] Check client messages (Slack, email, GHL)
- [ ] Respond to urgent items only (batch others)
- [ ] Review today's scheduled calls
- [ ] Check campaign performance (red flags only)

**9:00 AM - Proactive Outreach**
- [ ] Send 20+ cold emails (use Instantly templates)
- [ ] Send 10 LinkedIn connection requests + messages
- [ ] Follow up with warm leads from previous days
- [ ] Update CRM with notes

### Afternoon Routine (2-3 hours)

**12:00 PM - Client Work Block 1**
- [ ] Highest priority client deliverables
- [ ] Campaign optimizations
- [ ] Content creation

**3:00 PM - Client Work Block 2**
- [ ] Secondary client tasks
- [ ] Schedule social media posts
- [ ] Write blog content

### Evening Routine (30 min)

**5:00 PM - Planning & Admin**
- [ ] Update task list for tomorrow
- [ ] Log time worked per client (for profitability tracking)
- [ ] Process inbox to zero
- [ ] Quick review of metrics

```

1. What is your business name and website?
2. What services/products do you offer?
3. What makes you different from competitors?
4. Who is your ideal customer? (age, location, income, etc.)
5. What geographic area do you serve?

GOALS & CHALLENGES
6. What are your top 3 business goals for the next 12 months?
7. What's your biggest marketing challenge right now?
8. What would success look like 6 months from now?
9. How many new customers/clients do you want per month?

CURRENT MARKETING
10. What marketing have you tried before? What worked/didn't work?
11. What's your current monthly marketing budget (excluding our services)?
12. Do you have any existing marketing assets (photos, videos, testimonials)?

ACCESS NEEDED
13. Please provide login access to:
    - Google Business Profile
    - Google Ads (if applicable)
    - Meta Business Suite / Facebook
    - Website admin / CMS
    - Google Analytics
    - Any other marketing tools

CONTENT & BRAND
14. What is your brand's personality? (professional, friendly, luxury, etc.)
15. What topics are off-limits or sensitive for your industry?
16. Who is authorized to approve content before posting?
```

## Service Delivery SOPs

### Google Business Profile Management

**Weekly Tasks (30 min/client):**
1. Create 1 Google Post (update, offer, event, or article)
2. Respond to all new reviews within 24 hours
3. Answer any Q&A questions
4. Check for and correct any profile edits by Google
5. Add new photos if available

**Monthly Tasks (1 hour/client):**
1. Audit profile completeness
2. Update services/products if changed
3. Review and update business description for keywords
4. Check competitor profiles for ideas
5. Generate review request campaign

**Review Response Templates:**

5-Star Review:
```
Thank you so much, [Name]! We're thrilled you had a great experience
with [specific thing they mentioned]. We look forward to seeing you again!
```

3-4 Star Review:
```
Thank you for your feedback, [Name]. We're glad you enjoyed [positive aspect]
and we appreciate your input about [area for improvement]. We're always
working to improve, and we'd love to make your next visit even better.
```

1-2 Star Review:
```
[Name], we're sorry to hear your experience didn't meet expectations.
This isn't the standard we hold ourselves to. Please reach out to us at
[email/phone] so we can make this right. We value your feedback and
hope to have the opportunity to serve you better.
```

## Sales Process SOPs

### Lead Follow-Up Cadence

**Day 0:** Initial inquiry comes in
- Respond within 1 hour during business hours
- Send calendar link to book discovery call

**Day 1:** If no response
- Follow up email: "Just making sure you saw this"

**Day 3:** If no response
- Follow up with additional value (case study, tip)

**Day 7:** If no response
- "Are you still interested?" email

**Day 14:** Final follow up
- "I'll assume the timing isn't right" email (creates urgency)

**Day 30+:** Add to nurture sequence
- Monthly value emails until they convert or unsubscribe

---

### Discovery Call Script

**Opening (2 min)**
"Hey [Name], thanks for taking the time to chat. Before we dive in, can you give me a quick overview of [Business Name] and what you're hoping to achieve?"

**Situation Questions (5 min)**
- "How long have you been in business?"
- "How are you currently getting new customers?"
- "What marketing are you doing now, if any?"
- "Who handles your marketing currently?"

**Pain Questions (8 min)**
- "What's your biggest challenge when it comes to getting new customers?"
- "What have you tried before that didn't work?"
- "What's that costing you? In revenue, time, stress?"
- "What would it mean for your business if you could solve this?"

**Goal Questions (5 min)**
- "Where do you want to be in 12 months?"
- "How many new customers per month would make a real difference?"
- "What does your ideal marketing situation look like?"

**Transition to Solution (3 min)**
"Based on what you've shared, here's what I think would work best for you..." [Tailor recommendation to their specific situation]

**Pricing & Close (7 min)**
- Present recommended package
- Explain what's included and why
- Share relevant case study
- Ask: "Do you have any questions?"
- Ask: "Would you like to move forward?"

## Financial Operations

### Pricing & Invoicing Rules

1. **Always require payment upfront** - Invoice on the 1st, due immediately
2. **Retainers are non-refundable** - They pay for the month ahead
3. **One-time projects: 50% upfront, 50% on completion**
4. **Late payments: Send reminder Day 3, final warning Day 7, pause work Day 10**
5. **No work starts without signed contract and first payment**

### Client Payment Setup

- Stripe for credit cards (2.9% + $0.30 per transaction)
- ACH bank transfers for larger clients (lower fees)
- PayPal as backup option
- Set up autopay in GoHighLevel when possible

### Monthly Bookkeeping Checklist

- [ ] Categorize all transactions
- [ ] Reconcile bank accounts
- [ ] Send all invoices
- [ ] Follow up on unpaid invoices
- [ ] Record contractor payments
- [ ] Set aside 25-30% for taxes
- [ ] Review profit margin by client

## Emergency Procedures

### Client Emergency (their business problem)
1. Acknowledge immediately
2. Assess: Is this actually urgent or can it wait?
3. If urgent: Fix now, communicate updates
4. If not urgent: Set expectations for timeline
5. Document what happened and how it was resolved

### Your Emergency (personal, illness, etc.)
1. Notify affected clients ASAP
2. Reschedule any calls
3. Pause any active campaigns if needed
4. Have backup plans documented
5. Consider contractor backup for emergencies

### Reputation Emergency (bad review, PR issue)
1. Don't respond immediately - take 10 minutes
2. Screenshot everything
3. Draft response, have someone review
4. Respond professionally (never defensively)
5. Take conversation offline if needed
